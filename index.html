Textile Traditions

Discover the symbolic patterns of kasena textiles, where geometric designs represent clan identities and natural elements like rain and fertility.

The weaving techniques used are among the oldest in West Africa, with patterns that tell stories of migration, harvest, and community values. Natural dyes are extracted from plants and minerals found in the local environment.

Pikworo Slave Camp

A historical site that served as a slave camp during the trans-Atlantic slave trade, preserving the memory of this painful period.

  • Established in 1704 as a slave transit camp
  • Features natural rock formations used as prison cells
  • Guided tours explaining the history and significance
  • Annual remembrance ceremonies
Tongo Hills

Sacred hills featuring unique rock formations and ancient shrines central to Kasena spiritual practices.

  • Site of annual Damba festival celebrations
  • Ancient rock shrines for ancestral worship
  • Panoramic views of the surrounding landscape
  • Guided spiritual tours available

About the Museum

Preserving and celebrating the rich heritage of the Kasena people

Our History

The Kasena Heritage Museum was established in 1998 through a collaborative effort between the Kasena Traditional Council and the Ghanaian Ministry of Culture.

Our mission is to preserve, document, and showcase the cultural heritage of the Kasena people - one of Ghana's most historically significant ethnic groups.

The museum complex spans 5 acres and includes exhibition halls, a research center, an amphitheater for cultural performances, and a traditional compound demonstrating authentic Kasena architecture.

Cultural Preservation

We work closely with Kasena elders and community leaders to ensure the authenticity of our exhibits and programs. Our collections include over 2,000 artifacts representing various aspects of Kasena life, from agricultural tools to ceremonial objects.

Our Mission

To serve as a living repository of Kasena heritage, fostering cultural continuity by connecting past traditions with contemporary expressions, and promoting intercultural understanding through education and engagement.
